# How to Kill This

This is a standing invitation. The two-sorted arithmetic includes its own kill switch.

---

## The Claim

The absorbing element structure at the boundary of every sufficiently powerful formal system is isomorphic, mappable onto the same abstract structure via a structure-preserving morphism that:

1. Maps boundary to boundary: `φ(e₁) = e₂`
2. Commutes with operations: `φ(f₁(x, e₁)) = f₂(φ(x), e₂)`
3. Preserves the Origin|Bounded distinction

## How to Falsify It

Find a formal system where:

- There is a well-defined boundary condition (an operation that "leaves" the domain)
- The boundary does NOT satisfy the interaction axioms (I1–I3)
- OR the morphism to any existing domain fails one of the three properties above

One counterexample kills the claim.

## What Does NOT Falsify It

- "I don't like the axioms", the axioms are proven forced (`forced_interaction_axioms`), not chosen
- "The Lean proofs only show consistency", correct, and the document says so explicitly
- "The physics sections are speculative", correct, and the document labels them as such
- "You just defined things to work out", address `forced_interaction_axioms` specifically

## What Would

- A formal system with a boundary that demonstrably does not satisfy I1–I3
- A proof that two verified domains have structurally different absorbing behavior
- A second valid split of zero (not into Origin|Bounded but into some other categorically distinct pair) that is equally consistent
